Species

 

The old app species lists are completely useless for our purposes. We need much more specific, standard, official names. There are standard codes from the New Zealand Organisms Register (NZOR). This is the purpose! https://www.nzor.org.nz So we should use it.

Short Codes

Each name has an entry with the source discoverer and lots of other technical details. The GIUD is not very friendly so I have adopted the shortcode format widely used for plants. This is the first three characters of the latin short name. It will have a few duplicates but this does not matter for the limited list we need. Ambiguity can be handled by keeping the full latin PartialName in the table and adjusting the shortname. We are only interested in the species rank, not sub-species or genus. Sometimes it may be useful such as Mustelids or Rattus.

 

Here’s a justification for using short codes instead of NZOR GUIDs:

 

1. Human Readability

 

Short codes like ACACHL are easy to interpret and verify visually.

Field staff, analysts, and GIS users can quickly recognize species without needing to cross-reference a GUID.

 

2. Practicality in Mapping & Labeling

 

Short codes fit well in map labels, legends, and attribute tables where space is limited.

GUIDs are long, opaque identifiers that clutter maps and are not meaningful to most users.

 

3. Workflow Efficiency

 

Short codes can be generated dynamically from species partial names using QGIS expressions or scripts.

No need for external lookups or maintaining a separate NZOR reference table.

 

4. Portability

 

Short codes remain useful across different systems and exports (CSV, shapefiles, reports).

GUIDs often require a live link to NZOR or a lookup table to be meaningful.

 

5. Error Reduction

 

When editing or filtering data manually, short codes reduce the risk of mistakes compared to copying long GUID strings.

 

This is the list extracted as an initial lookup table called bird_pest_lut. We can add or subtract extra records. Since the shortcode is the key, we can change the common name as we wish but these are generally the names from NZOR except Hedgehog.

The PartialName is the accepted latin name and the first_name is the most used common name. We could also get alternative Māori names as a second column. The shortcode is the key. I have added extra birds as they are seen, eg Greenfinch.
